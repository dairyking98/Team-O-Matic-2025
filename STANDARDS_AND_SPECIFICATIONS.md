## Li+ Battery Relevant Standards

### OSHA Standards (29 CFR 1910)

- **Subpart L – Fire Protection**: Establishes requirements for fire prevention, detection, and suppression systems in workplaces.

- **Subpart S – Electrical**: Covers safe design, installation, and maintenance of electrical systems to prevent shocks, burns, and fires.

- **1910.39 – Fire Prevention Plans**: Requires employers to develop and implement written fire prevention plans.

- **1910.119 – Process Safety Management**: Controls hazards associated with highly hazardous chemicals, relevant when Li-ion manufacturing involves reactive materials.

- **1910.120 – Hazardous Waste Operations and Emergency Response**: Governs worker protection during cleanup or response to hazardous material releases.

- **1910.132 – Personal Protective Equipment**: Mandates assessment and provision of suitable PPE for workplace hazards.

- **1910.134 – Respiratory Protection**: Requires proper respirators and programs when airborne contaminants are present.

- **1910.147 – The Control of Hazardous Energy (Lockout/Tagout)**: Ensures energy sources are isolated during servicing of machinery.

- **1910.151 – Medical Services and First Aid**: Requires readily available medical and first aid support for employees.

- **1910.178(g)(ii), (iii), and (l) – Powered Industrial Trucks**: Specifies design, maintenance, and safe operation of forklifts and battery-powered trucks.

- **1910.1000 – Air Contaminants**: Limits employee exposure to airborne chemical hazards.

- **1910.1020 – Access to Exposure and Medical Records**: Ensures workers can review their health and exposure records.

- **1910.1200 – Hazard Communication**: Requires labeling, SDS, and training for hazardous chemicals including battery electrolytes.

### DOT / PHMSA / 49 CFR

- **49 CFR (Subparts under PHMSA)**: Regulates packaging, labeling, and transport of hazardous materials including lithium batteries to ensure public safety.

### Underwriters Laboratory (UL)

- **UL 1642 – Lithium Cell Safety**: Defines safety tests for lithium cells (short-circuit, crush, overcharge) to prevent thermal runaway.

### International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)

- **IEC 62485-6 – Traction Battery Safety**: Provides design, operation, and maintenance requirements for lithium batteries used in traction (vehicle) systems.

- **IEC 62619**: Specifies safety requirements and testing for industrial lithium-ion batteries.

## Electrical and Fire Safety Relevant Standards

- **NFPA 855 – Energy Storage Systems Installation**: Governs fire protection and installation requirements for stationary battery systems.

- **UL 9540 – Energy Storage Equipment**: Certifies complete energy-storage systems for electrical and fire safety compliance.

- **IEC 62281 – Transport Safety of Lithium Cells/Batteries**: Specifies procedures to prevent fire or explosion during battery transport.

- **NFPA 7 - National Electric Code**: Establishes safe electrical design, installation, and fire prevention practices.

## Powered Industrial Truck Relevant Standards

- **29 CFR 1910.178 – Industrial Trucks**: OSHA standard covering operator training, maintenance, and safe use of forklifts.

- **ANSI / ITSDF B56.1 – Safety Standard for Low Lift and High Lift Trucks**: Details design and operation safety for powered industrial trucks.

## Other Standards

- **ISO 26262 – Automotive Functional Safety**: Framework ensuring electrical/electronic systems in vehicles meet acceptable risk levels.

- **UN 38.3 / 49 CFR §173.185 – Transport of Lithium Batteries**: Mandates tests (shock, thermal, vibration, etc.) for safe shipment of lithium cells.

- **ISO 3691-4**: Defines safety requirements for driverless and semi-automated industrial trucks and related equipment.
